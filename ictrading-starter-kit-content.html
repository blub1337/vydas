Risk Management Checklist — Protect Your Capital

Professional traders don't make money by being right more often. They make money by managing risk so well that a losing streak doesn't wipe them out. This checklist is your safety net.

The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1% of your account on a single trade. This is non-negotiable. Here's how to calculate your position size:

Pre-Trade Checklist (Run This Before Every Trade)

  • ☐ Trend check: Is price above or below EMA 50/200? Trade only in trend direction.
  • ☐ Support/Resistance: Where are the nearest key levels? Place SL beyond them.
  • ☐ Risk/Reward: Is your RR at least 1:2? If not, skip the trade.
  • ☐ Spread check: Is the spread within normal range? Avoid trading during news spikes.
  • ☐ Position size: Does this trade risk ≤1% of your account? Recalculate if not.
  • ☐ News check: Is there a high-impact news event in the next 2 hours? If yes, wait.
⚠️ Hard Rule

If you can't answer YES to all 6 checklist items, don't take the trade. Missing one check is how accounts get blown. ICTrading's raw spreads mean you get fair execution — but no amount of tight spreads saves you from bad risk management.

Daily Risk Limits

  • Max daily loss: 3% of account. Hit it? Stop trading for the day.
  • Max consecutive losses: 3 trades. Hit it? Stop and review your strategy.
  • Max daily trades: 5 (for manual trading). Quality over quantity.

Strategy 1: The ICTrading Scalper (M5)

Best for: Traders who can sit at the screen for 2-3 hours. Timeframe: M5. Session: London open (07:00-10:00 UTC) or New York open (13:00-16:00 UTC).

Setup

  • Chart: M5 candlestick
  • Indicators: EMA 50, RSI (14)
  • Asset: XAUUSD (Gold) or EURUSD — tightest spreads on ICTrading

Entry Rules

  1. Trend filter: Price must be above EMA 50 for longs, below for shorts
  2. Momentum: RSI between 30-50 for longs (oversold bounce), 50-70 for shorts (overbought pullback)
  3. Entry: Wait for a bullish/bearish engulfing candle at EMA 50 touch
  4. Stop loss: 5 pips below/above the engulfing candle's low/high
  5. Take profit: 15 pips (1:3 risk/reward)

Strategy 2: Trend Following (H1)

Best for: Traders with a day job. Timeframe: H1. Session: Any, but best during London + New York overlap (13:00-17:00 UTC).

Setup

  • Chart: H1 candlestick
  • Indicators: EMA 50, EMA 200
  • Asset: Any major pair (EURUSD, GBPUSD, USDJPY) or indices (SPX500, NAS100)

Entry Rules

  1. Trend confirmation: EMA 50 must be above EMA 200 (uptrend) or below (downtrend)
  2. Pullback entry: Wait for price to pull back to EMA 50
  3. Confirmation candle: A bullish/bearish candle closing in trend direction at EMA 50
  4. Stop loss: 20 pips below/above the pullback candle
  5. Take profit: 60 pips (1:3 risk/reward) — or trail with EMA 50

When to Skip

  • During major news releases (NFP, FOMC, CPI)
  • When price is choppy between EMA 50 and EMA 200 (no clear trend)
  • During low-volatility hours (Asian session, 22:00-07:00 UTC)

Strategy 3: Breakout Trading (M15)

Best for: Traders who like clear, high-probability setups. Timeframe: M15. Session: London open or New York open — highest breakout probability.

Setup

  • Chart: M15 candlestick
  • Indicators: None needed — just support/resistance lines
  • Asset: Indices (SPX500, NAS100, DAX40) — they respect levels best

Entry Rules

  1. Identify range: Find a clear support and resistance level (at least 2 touches each)
  2. Wait for breakout: Price breaks above resistance or below support with a strong candle
  3. Retest entry: Enter on the first retest of the broken level (now acting as support/resistance)
  4. Stop loss: 10 pips inside the broken range
  5. Take profit: 30 pips (1:3 risk/reward) — or next major level
⚠️ False Breakout Filter

Only take the trade if the breakout candle closes beyond the level. A wick through is not a breakout. ICTrading's ECN execution means your entry is filled at the market price — no requotes, no slippage games.
